## What is Gang War?

**Gang War** is a gang-versus-gang PvP system built around **war stacks**, **milestones**, **war score**, and **war points**. Your gang builds momentum by chaining valid war kills, reaching streak milestones, and converting that pressure into score and tactical advantages.

## Where can I find Gang War?

You can access Gang War from your **Gang Details** page. The **War Panel** gives you a quick view of your gang's current war progress, and the dedicated Gang War page shows more detailed information like your current stack progress and war points.

## How does the war stack work?

The **war stack** tracks your gang's current streak of valid war kills.

- Each valid war kill adds **1** to your stack.
- Your stack timer refreshes after each valid war kill.
- If your gang goes too long without another valid war kill, the stack expires and resets.
- Higher stacks unlock better milestone rewards.

This system rewards sustained aggression and coordinated gang activity.

## What are milestones?

**Milestones** are reward checkpoints tied to your war stack. When your gang reaches one, you earn both **war score** and **war points**.

The higher your streak climbs, the better the rewards become. Your gang also receives a notification when a milestone is reached, so everyone can see the momentum swing.

## How does scoring work?

Your gang earns **war score** from milestone rewards, not from every individual kill.

- Early kills build your stack.
- Milestones convert that stack into score.
- Larger streaks lead to bigger score gains.

That means Gang War is really about maintaining streaks long enough to cash them in.

## What are war points?

**War points** are earned alongside war score whenever your gang reaches a milestone. Unlike war score, war points are a **spendable gang resource** used for war-related upgrades and deployments.

## What are war buffs?

**War buffs** are gang-wide bonuses that leaders can deploy using **war points** while your gang is in war mode. They are meant to strengthen your gang's performance during war, and stronger or overlapping buffs may affect which deployments are available.

You can manage them through the **War Quartermaster** on your gang's war page.

## What counts as a war kill?

A fight only advances Gang War if it qualifies as **war combat** and ends in a valid kill.

In practice, that means:

- both players must belong to different gangs
- both gangs must be in war mode
- the attacker must have war participation enabled 

If a fight does not meet those conditions, it can still behave like normal PvP, but it will not build your war stack or award war progress.

## What happens when I get killed in war?

If you lose a valid war fight, you are **hospitalized** and temporarily removed from combat. While hospitalized:

- you cannot take part in combat
- you cannot be used as a valid war target

The base recovery time is **2 hours**, but effects that reduce hospitalization time can shorten that recovery.

## Can I opt out of war?

Gang leaders can toggle a member's war participation on or off.

- There is a **1-hour cooldown** between participation changes for the same member.
- Turning participation off stops that member from contributing war progress as an attacker.
- It does **not** make that member untargetable while the gang is still in war mode.

If your gang is in war, enemies can still attack you even when your own participation is disabled.

## How do I enable or disable war mode?

Only the **gang leader** can toggle war mode.

1. Open your **Gang Details** page.
2. Open the gang menu.
3. Choose **Enable War Mode** or **Disable War Mode**.

Once war mode is turned on, it stays locked for **24 hours** before it can be turned off again.

Disabling war mode is a full reset of your current war run. It clears your gang's **war score**, **war points**, **war stack**, and any active **war buffs** tied to war status.

## What happens to war score over time?

War score naturally **decays** over time, so inactive gangs slowly lose momentum.

If you want to hold your ranking, you need steady war activity.

## Can I attack my own gang members?

You can still attack your own gang members through normal PvP actions, but it does **not** count as Gang War progress. Only fights against rival gangs can build your war stack and trigger milestones.

## Who can participate in Gang War?

Gang War participation depends on both gang state and member state.

- Your gang must be in war mode.
- The attacking member must have war participation enabled.
- The fight must qualify as valid war combat.

If those conditions are not met, the fight will not contribute to war progression.

## What is the engagement lock?

When you take part in war combat as an attacker, you trigger a **24-hour engagement lock**.

During that lock, you are treated as recently engaged in war and cannot simply detach from the consequences of that action. This prevents players from attacking in war and then immediately dodging retaliation through gang management actions.

## Which gang actions are blocked during war?

Gang War places extra restrictions on gang management.

- A member who has recently engaged in war cannot **leave** the gang until the engagement lock expires.
- A member who has recently engaged in war cannot be **kicked** during that lock.
- A gang cannot be **disbanded** while war mode is active.
- Even after war mode is turned off, disbanding can still be blocked until recent war engagement locks have expired.

These restrictions are there to stop gangs from abusing exits, kicks, or disbands to avoid war consequences.

## How do I check my war status?

Your war status appears in a few different places:

- The **War Panel** on your Gang Details page shows your current **war score** and **stack**.
- The dedicated Gang War page shows your broader war overview, including **war points**.
- Member-specific participation status and cooldown information can be checked from the gang member interface.

Watching these screens helps you understand whether your gang is building momentum or losing it.

## Where do I see milestone progress?

You can track milestone progress on the dedicated Gang War page. It includes a milestone view that shows your current stack, which rewards you have already reached, and what milestone comes next.

That makes it easier to judge whether your gang should press the attack or regroup before the stack expires.
 
## Any tips for Gang War?

- **Chain your wins**: Gang War rewards streaks, so protecting your stack matters more than chasing random fights.
- **Watch your timer**: If your stack expires, your gang has to rebuild from the start.
- **Track milestone progress**: Use the Gang War page to see how close you are to the next reward.
- **Do not confuse opt-out with safety**: Turning participation off does not stop enemies from targeting you while your gang is still in war mode.
- **Plan before disabling war mode**: Turning war mode off wipes your current war progress.
